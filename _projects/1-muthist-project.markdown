Genomes are the substrate upon which evolutionary history is written. Life on Earth today is the result of the accumulation of billions of years of mutations and the acceptance or rejection of those mutations through processes of selection. However, even though mutation is the fundamental source of variation in biology, questions of precisely how mutational history plays out across genome evolution still remain open.

We use the MABE digital evolution framework to answer questions about mutational history in digital genomes, including:
* Are certain classes of mutations (e.g., insertion-deletion mutations, point mutations, copy number mutations) favored over others early in evolution? Later?  
* At what rates do these mutations fix?
* How do fixation rates relate to mutation rates? 
* etc. 

This is ongoing work; our preliminary results will (pending acceptance!) be presented at the ALIFE 2019 conference in Newcastle, UK.
